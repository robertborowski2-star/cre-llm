ACM ADVISORS - INTERNAL GLOSSARY OF TERMS
==========================================
DEAL PROCESS & DOCUMENTS
--------------------------
PAC ([COMPANY])
[initial loan presentation document created by an analyst and assessed by the investment committee.]
[COMPANY] ([COMPANY])
[final loan presentation document for approval and final sign off, commitment letter issuance and ultimate funding if approved]
Term Sheet
[initial non-legally binding presentation of terms to a [COMPANY]]
Commitment Letter
[final legally binding document outlining all terms and conditions of the loan/financing which governs all future items related to the loan]
LOAN STRUCTURE TERMS
---------------------
[COMPANY]
[senior debt position with registartion on title that ranks ahead of other debt and all other equity ]
Second Mortgage / Mezzanine
[second ranking debt registered on title]
A-Note / B-Note
[a rank split within an existing overall mortgage that can be 1st or 2nd ranking on title. Its a subdivision of debt within an mortgage]
Construction Holdback
[regulatory holdbacks as part of construction financing. Not necessarily just related to debt but all construction projects where the provinicial government requires the builder/developer to holdback some amount of the total cost in the event of a lien]
Equity Holdback
[funds held back in the event of an unforeseen issue usually negotiated between buyer and seller]
UNDERWRITING METRICS
---------------------
DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio)
[ratio of how much cashflow there is available for every 1 dollar of debt payments]
[COMPANY] (Loan to Value)
[a ratio showing how much debt there is with respect to the total value of the asset]
GDS / [COMPANY]
[Gross Debt Service and [COMPANY]; same as DSCR but ratio is reversed and typically only used in single family residential lending]
NOI (Net Operating Income)
[Income of a property before debt payments]
[PERSON]
[a ratio of how much income is generated related to the value of the property - NOI/value = cap rate]
[COMPANY]
[GOC is government of Canada bond - the spread is how much a lender charges above that bond yield which equates to the interest rate the borrower pays]
Debt Yield
[like cap rate but [COMPANY]/loan amount - tells lender how much they can yield on their debt if the income remains]

PROPERTY [COMPANY]
--------------
MFAM (Multifamily)
[apartment buildings]
[COMPANY] (Industrial, Commercial, Institutional)
