Application Highlights
CBRE Capital (Canada) Inc. (“CBRE”), has been exclusively retained by HNC 500 Block I LP, by its general partner, HNC 500
[COMPANY] and HNC 500 Block I HT Inc. (the “Borrower”) to arrange financing options for their best-in-class purpose built rental
asset, known as One [LOCATION], located at 510 12th Avenue SW in the heart of Calgary’s Beltline district (“One Park Central” or
the “Property”). [COMPANY] is requesting both conventional term financing and CMHC Insured term financing under the MLI Market
program options. The [COMPANY] is currently encumbered by a $115,000,000 conventional loan which will be paid out with one of
the scenarios below. The [COMPANY] comprises one, 32-storey concrete framed building, totaling 462 apartment units and 8,488 SF
of fully occupied ground floor retail space. The [COMPANY] features 48 bachelor units, 294 one-bedroom units, 116 two-bedroom
units, and 4 three-bedroom penthouse units.
The [COMPANY] was developed and continues to be managed by [COMPANY], one of the largest privately held real estate investors and
managers in the world. Founded in 1957, [COMPANY]’ reputation for excellence has garnered the trust of leading investors, clients, and
partners in 30 countries, maximizing its outreach and transforming the urban landscapes in the global real estate market. As of
June 30, 2025, [COMPANY]’ portfolio consists of US$91.8 billion of assets under management and includes 832 properties,
representing over 288 million square feet of real estate assets across the risk spectrum and all property types.
The expected funding timeline for [COMPANY] option is March 2026, whereas the expected funding timeline for
conventional financing is January 2026.
[COMPANY] will award the non-recourse financing opportunity to the prospective lender that will match the [COMPANY]’s preferences
most closely. The [COMPANY]’s goal is to finance One [LOCATION] at the lowest available interest rate reflecting the premier
quality of both the asset and its location, the world-class sponsorship and the very conservative loan to value of approximately
65%. The [COMPANY]’s preferences for each financing option are as follows:
1. CMHC MLI Market: Effectiveness and experience dealing with CMHC and meeting expectations with regards to the
funding timeline, specifically timeline to submit to CMHC is important. Additional preference will be given to the lender that
can offer the most competitive pricing and fees for a 5-year term.
2. Conventional Financing: Meeting expectations with regards to the funding timeline. Additional preference will be given to
the lender that can offer competitive pricing, along with a 30-year amortization or longer, and interest-only payments
options for all, or part, of the 5-year term.
