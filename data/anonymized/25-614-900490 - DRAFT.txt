DISCOUNTED CASH FLOW APPROACH
METHODOLOGY
The basic premise of this method is that the present worth of the property equals the present value of the future
benefits. These benefits represent the net annual cash flow - positive or negative - over a given period of time,
plus the net proceeds from the forecast resale.
Based upon empirical data, the projected rental rates are inflated over the period of the projection to develop a
forecast of the probable income and expenses over the term of the investment and it is assumed that the property
will be sold at the end of the projection period with the future value is discounted back to the present time. A time
horizon of 11 years has been employed in the present instance, (since this time frame remains the typical
employed by investors), with an assumed sale of the property in the 12th year.
The existing leases are analyzed on a tenant-by-tenant basis and a projection is made over the holding period;
rental data is broken down to a price per square foot basis/per annum equivalent. At the end of the existing lease
terms, the individual rents are automatically increased to present market levels (plus inflation) at a selected rate.
VALUATION CRITERIA
The Discounted Cash Flow Approach incorporates three critical assumptions:
1. The rate of inflation applicable to market rentals, tenant sales and expenses;
2. The capitalization rate used to convert the net income stream in the last year of the investment into a value
estimate;
3. The discount rate/internal rate of return to be applied to the net operating income stream/reversionary value.
An analysis of the investment market across Canada indicates that, over the long term, investors are utilizing a
long-term inflation factor of between 1.5% and 3.0%. In this case, an average growth factor of 2% per annum was
applied to market rents over the holding period. Operating costs were also inflated by 2% per annum.
The overall capitalization rate vis-à-vis the last year's income stream (terminal capitalization rate) is perceived to
be 7.50%, which is 25 bps higher than the [COMPANY] used in [COMPANY] (see previous section).
The majority of institutional purchasers are currently motivated by [COMPANY] ([COMPANY]) which is based
on the equivalent yield for long term bonds and mortgages plus a premium for risk liquidity and management
expertise. Based on our survey of yields and other investments during the past year, a realistic present discount
rate is deemed to be between 8.00% and 8.50% for the subject property. This discount rate is based on a
