3. Summary of significant accounting policies
Investment properties
Investment properties are comprised of properties held to earn rent or for capital appreciation, or both.
The Fund accounts for its investment properties using the fair value model. Investment properties are
initially measured at cost, including transaction costs if the transaction is deemed to be an asset
acquisition. Transaction costs include commissions, land transfer taxes, and professional fees for legal
and other services directly related to the acquisition.
Subsequent to initial recognition, investment properties are re-measured at fair value, which reflects
market conditions at the reporting date. The carrying value of investment properties includes all capital
expenditures associated with upgrading and extending the economic life of the existing properties. Gains
or losses arising from changes in the fair values of investment properties are included in net income in
the period in which they arise. Fair value is supported by valuations using market-based assumptions
and independent external valuations, each in accordance with recognized valuation techniques. The
fair value of investment properties are determined by real estate valuation professionals using
recognized valuation techniques under the principles of IFRS 13 Fair Value Measurement.
Investment properties are derecognized when either they have been disposed of or when it is
permanently withdrawn from use and no future economic benefit is expected from their disposal. Any
gains or losses on the retirement or disposal of an investment property is recognized in net income in
the period of retirement or disposal.

Fair value measurements
The Fund measures its investment properties, mortgages payable, and derivatives at fair value at each
reporting date. Fair value is the price that would be received to sell an asset or paid to transfer a liability
in an orderly transaction between market participants at the measurement date. The fair value
measurement is based on the presumption that the transaction to sell the asset or transfer the liability
takes place either:
• In the principal market for the asset or liability; or
• In the absence of a principal market, in the most advantageous market for the asset or liability.
All assets and liabilities, for which fair value is measured or disclosed in the consolidated financial
statements are categorized within the fair value hierarchy, based on the lowest level input that is
significant to the fair value measurement as a whole:
• Level 1 — Quoted (unadjusted) market prices in active markets for identical assets or liabilities.
• Level 2 — Valuation techniques for which the lowest level input that is significant to the fair
value measurement is directly or indirectly observable.
• Level 3 — Valuation techniques for which the lowest level input that is significant to the fair
value measurement is unobservable.
For assets and liabilities that are recognized in the consolidated financial statements at fair value on a
recurring basis, the Fund determines whether transfers have occurred between levels in the hierarchy
by re-assessing categorization (based on the lowest level input that is significant to the fair value
measurement as a whole) at the end of each reporting period.

Valuation methodology and process
The values of real estate investments are prepared giving consideration to the income, cost, and sales
comparison approaches of estimating property values. The income approach estimates an income stream
for a property (typically ten years) and discounts this income plus a reversion (presumed sale) value into
a present value at a risk adjusted rate. Yield rates and growth assumptions utilized in this approach are
derived from market transactions as well as other financial and industry data. The cost approach estimates
the replacement cost of the building less physical depreciation plus the land value. Generally, this
approach provides a check on the value derived using the income approach. The sales comparison
approach compares recent transactions to the appraised property. Adjustments are made for
dissimilarities, which typically provide a range of value.
The entrepreneurial profit approach is calculated based on the stabilized value of the finalized
development, less the cost to develop. Stabilized value is calculated using a reversion cap rate which was
5.75% at December 31, 2021. Ten percent of entrepreneurial profit is allocated to construction risk and is
earned during construction based on percentage completed at time of valuation. Construction risk and
reversion cap rate are key assumptions used in calculating entrepreneurial profit.
Many factors are also considered in the determination of fair value, including, but not limited to, the
operating cash flows and financial performance of the properties relative to budgets or projections,
property types and geographic locations, the physical condition of the assets, prevailing market
capitalization rates, prevailing market discount rates, general economic conditions, economic conditions
specific to the market in which the assets are located, expected exit timing and strategy, and any specific
rights or terms associated with the investment. Because of the inherent uncertainties of valuation, the
values reflected in the consolidated financial statements may materially differ from the values that would
be determined by negotiations held between parties in a sale transaction.
For each calendar year, the Fund follows a valuation policy whereby investment values are determined
quarterly by one self-contained appraisal report for the quarter containing the anniversary date of each
acquisition and three limited restricted appraisals for the remaining three quarters, in accordance with the
Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice. The limited restricted appraisals include less

7. Derivatives
The Fund has entered into interest rate swap derivative contracts to manage interest rate risk exposure.
The Fund uses these agreements to reduce its exposure to increases in interest costs under certain
variable rate indebtedness. The interest rate swap agreements involve the receipt of variable rate
amounts in exchange for fixed rate interest payments over the life of the agreements without an exchange
of the underlying principal amount. Net amounts received or paid pursuant to the interest rate swaps
recognized as a decrease or increase to interest expense when such amounts are earned, respectively.
The Fund applies hedge accounting to its interest rate swap contracts and recognizes any change in the
fair value of such instruments through other comprehensive income on the consolidated statement of
income and comprehensive income.
For the period May 20, 2021 (commencement of operations) through December 31, 2021, the Fund
recognized the change in the value of its interest rate swap contracts of $807,764 as a component of other
comprehensive loss within the consolidated statement of income and comprehensive income. As of
December 31, 2021, approximately $145,279,925 of the mortgages payable were hedged with
corresponding interest rate swaps. The average effective interest rate for the Fund’s mortgages payable
hedged with interest rate swaps was 3.21%.

Credit risk
Credit risk is the risk that a counterparty will not meet its obligations under a financial instrument or
customer contract, leading to a financial loss. The Fund is exposed to credit risk on all financial assets
and its exposure is generally limited to the carrying value of the financial asset. The Fund is exposed
to credit risk from the possibility that tenants may experience financial difficulty and be unable to fulfill
their lease term commitments. The Fund seeks to mitigate the risk of credit loss with respect to
tenants by obtaining a lease guarantor and security deposits from each residential tenant and
diversification of its existing portfolio of investment properties. The Fund monitors its collection process
on a monthly basis and ensures that a stringent policy is adopted to provide for all past due amounts.
