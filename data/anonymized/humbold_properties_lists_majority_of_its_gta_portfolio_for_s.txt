([PERSON]) After listing two major assets last year, Toronto-based developer [COMPANY] has listed an additional eight properties for sale, according to a CBRE marketing brochure obtained by RENX . On its website, [PERSON] states that it is a family-owned development company with over 50 years of experience, two offices in [COMPANY], and a portfolio across a diverse assortment of asset classes. With the new listing, however, most of the company’s portfolio is now on the market. The eight-property portfolio was listed by [COMPANY] on an unpriced basis and comprises 647,544 sq. ft. of space — industrial, retail, and office — that can be purchased together or separately. “The Humbold GTA Commercial Portfolio spans 8 properties in core [COMPANY] submarkets that offer a blend of cash flow security and value upside,” CBRE states in its sales brochure. “Overall, the [CITY] is 92% leased to 93 tenants across a wide variety of uses and industries, underscoring the defensive and diverse nature of the portfolio income. "Simultaneously, investors have access to value-add strategies including marking expiring rents to market, vacant lease up, conversion to condominium units, and infill development.” RENX reached out to [PERSON] this week to inquire about the listings, but has not received a response. Industrial Humbold’s website lists four industrial properties and all are now on the market. The largest property is 5781-5789 Steeles Ave. W. in Toronto, a 5.2-acre site with 129,542 sq. ft. of net leasable area that’s currently fully occupied to three tenants, according to [COMPANY]’s sales brochure. It also notes the property has long-term infill redevelopment potential. Also listed are two industrial properties in [CITY]. The first is 80-85 Citizen Ct., a 7.4-acre industrial business park with 106,753 sq. ft. of space that’s currently 98 per cent occupied by 23 tenants. The second is 4118 14th Ave., a 4.7-acre property with 68,489 sq. ft. of space leased to two tenants. Although the property is treated as a separate property, it is located directly adjacent to 80-85 Citizen Ct. Listed separately last year by [COMPANY] is 455 [PERSON]. in Toronto, a 3.50-acre industrial property with 87,273 sq. ft. of space that was previously occupied by [COMPANY]. The current asking price is $29,388,000 . Retail On the retail side, the largest listed property is 3700-3900 Rutherford Rd. and 9311-9333 Weston Rd. in [CITY], a 6.6-acre retail plaza known as [COMPANY] that’s home to 64,534 sq. ft. of space currently 100 per cent leased to nine tenants, including [COMPANY] and Scotiabank. [COMPANY] notes the property was constructed in 2004 and is a grocery-anchored shopping centre free and clear of existing financing. The other big property is the Dixie Aimco Plaza at 1510-1530 Aimco Blvd. in [CITY]. The plaza is home to 37,754 sq. ft. of space that’s fully leased to three tenants, including [PERSON]. This site is also free and clear of financing and [COMPANY] notes it has future intensification potential. Also listed is 125-127 Spadina Ave. in Toronto, a small retail property between Richmond Street West and Adelaide Street West constructed in 1900 with 5,084 sq. ft. of space. 125 [PERSON] is occupied by [COMPANY], while 127 [PERSON] is vacant. Mixed-use and development The largest asset that has been listed, across all asset classes, is 1118-1126 Finch Ave. [PERSON], a 9.8-acre mixed-use complex comprised of an office building — home to one of Humbold’s offices — and multiple standalone industrial buildings with office and retail space. According to [COMPANY], the complex was constructed in the late 1980s, has a total of 235,388 sq. ft. of space that is currently 79 per cent leased to 52 tenants with a weighted average lease term of 3.3 years. The last of the eight properties listed by [COMPANY] is 217 Adelaide St. W., a rectangular surface parking lot one block north from [PERSON]. For this site, [PERSON] has received [COMPANY] ([COMPANY]) approval to develop a 22-storey office building with 198,954 sq. ft of space. For this site, [PERSON] had also secured interim [COMPANY] approval for a multi-phased, mixed-use development with four towers between 38 and 60 storeys and 1,966,276 sq. ft of buildable area. [PERSON] noted the vendor was open to both a partial or full ownership interest sale.
