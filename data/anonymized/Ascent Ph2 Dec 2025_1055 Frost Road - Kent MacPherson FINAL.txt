cash, or in other precisely revealed terms, for which the specified property rights

should sell after reasonable exposure in a competitive market under all conditions
requisite to a fair sale, with the buyer and the seller each acting prudently,
knowledgeably, and for self-interest, and assuming that neither is under undue
duress."
Implicit in the definition is the consummation of a sale as of a specified date and the passing of title from
seller to buyer under conditions whereby:
• the buyer and seller are typically motivated;
• both parties are well informed or well advised, and acting in what they consider their
best interests;
• a reasonable time is allowed for exposure in the open market;
• payment is made in terms of cash in Canadian dollars or in terms of financial
arrangements comparable thereto; and
• the price represents the normal consideration for the property sold unaffected by
special or creative financing or sales concessions granted by anyone associated with
the sale.
The definition of market value prescribed in Section 32 of [COMPANY] is:
"The market value of an estate or interest in land is the amount that would have been
paid for it if it had been sold at the date of expropriation in the open market by a
willing seller to a willing buyer."
In summary, it is important to reiterate that the valuation of the subject assumes that the subject would
not be affected by any undue stimulus, with no consideration of a partial or whole disposition of the
assets under duress or forced sale conditions.

value. The exposure period, which is presumed to precede the date of effective date of the appraisal, is
defined as:
"The estimated length of time the property interest being appraised would have been
offered on the market before the hypothetical consummation of a sale at the estimated
value on [COMPANY] of the appraisal … Exposure time is backward-looking."6
The concept of reasonable market exposure encompasses not only adequate, sufficient and reasonable
time but also adequate, sufficient and reasonable effort. Known exposure time for the comparable indices
considered herein is ±12 months. The exposure period for the subject property, terminating at the
effective date under current market conditions, is estimated to be ±12 months for the scenario considered
herein.

4.6 SUMMARY AND CONCLUSIONS
The residential market experienced a decline in sales volume across all subtypes in 2022, continuing
through 2023 and 2024, generally returning to pre-COVID levels. This reduction in sales volume has
contributed to a decrease in total sales dollars from year-end 2023 to year-end 2024. Although market
demand was lower at the end of 2024 compared to the previous year, the first two quarters of 2025
showed stronger activity when compared to the same period in 2024. The increase in active inventory

[COMPANY] has been utilized herein to estimate the market value of the subject

A common method of valuation of rental apartment buildings is [COMPANY]. The
application of [COMPANY] requires the development of an opinion of market value
by comparing properties similar to the subject project that have sold recently, are listed for sale, or are
under contract.
[COMPANY] is defined by [COMPANY] as:
"A set of procedures in which an appraiser derives a value indication by comparing the
property being appraised to similar properties that have been sold recently, applying
appropriate units of comparison, and making adjustments, based on the elements of
comparison, to the sale price of the comparables."
The unit of comparison, which is defined as the component into which a property may be most logically
divided for comparison purposes.
The most commonly employed market indicator is the rate per suite which is simply derived by dividing
the sale or list price by the total number of units in the comparable property. This method is susceptible
to a wide dispersion in unit values because it does not account for suite mix. The sale price per room
indicator, based on Canada Mortgage and [COMPANY] room count formula, is superior to the
suite indicator because it accounts for the differences in unit mix. Based on the following formula:
• Studio/Bachelor Suite = 2.5 rooms

• Two Bedroom Suite = 4.5 rooms
• Two Bedroom Suite + Den = 5.0 rooms
• Three Bedroom Suite = 5.5 rooms
