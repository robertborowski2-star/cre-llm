It is customary, wherever applicable, to use the following approaches to value when appraising real
property:
(a) The Cost Approach -- requires the calculation of the replacement cost new of the
improvements at current rates less any accrued depreciation attributable to the present
improvements. This depreciation could result from physical, functional or locational
causes. To the depreciated value is added the estimated land value of the subject site.
The Cost Approach's main drawback is the difficulty of establishing an accurate estimate
of the various forms of depreciation. Further, it is rarely the basis on which properties
are purchased.
(b) [COMPANY] -- utilizes the method of comparing the subject
property with other properties of a similar nature which have sold on the open market
with adjustments being made to the sale price to compensate for any difference in size,
time of sale, utility and any special circumstances that may have had a bearing on the
price paid for them.
(c) The Income Approach
I) Direct Capitalization Method -- which is a process in which the net income derived
from the property is capitalized at an appropriate rate to indicate its capitalized value,
the assumption being that the capitalized value of the income stream is the present value
of the property producing the income.
ii) The Discounted Cash Flow Method -- this procedure is a variation of the Income
Approach. However, rather than basing the estimate of value on the stabilized net
operating income as is done by the conventional Income Approach, the Discounted Cash
Flow Method projects income and expenses for the property over a typical holding
period. At the end of this holding period, the property is assumed to be sold. The total
of the cash flows received over the holding period, as well as the moneys received from
the sale of the property, is then discounted to present day at a discount rate consistent
with that shown by the market place. The total of these discounted cash flows and the
reversionary value of the property represents a market value estimate of the property.
